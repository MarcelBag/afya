The Limits of Large Language Models

LeCun argues that LLMs are not a path to genuine intelligence. Despite their impressive outputs, they operate by predicting the next token in a sequence. This means they have no persistent memory across conversations, no grounded understanding of the physical world, no reliable causal reasoning, and no ability to plan sequences of actions toward a goal.

  • They hallucinate facts with high confidence
  • They cannot learn continuously from new experience
  • They lack any internal world model
  • They are brittle outside their training distribution
🔮

Why JEPA Is the Way Forward

LeCun's proposed alternative is the Joint Embedding Predictive Architecture (JEPA), a model that learns by predicting abstract representations of the world rather than raw tokens or pixels. Instead of asking "what word comes next?", JEPA asks "what is a plausible abstract state of the world given what I observe?"

This allows the model to build an internal world model, reason about cause and effect, plan ahead, and generalise far beyond its training data. It is how LeCun believes machines will eventually reach and surpass human-level intelligence.

🧩

What Exactly Is JEPA?

Joint Embedding Predictive Architecture

A Joint Embedding Predictive Architecture is a self-supervised learning framework introduced by LeCun and his team at Meta AI. Rather than reconstructing raw inputs (like pixels or tokens), it trains two encoder networks to produce abstract representations of an input and a prediction target, then learns to make those representations agree.

The key insight is that prediction in abstract space is far richer than prediction in raw signal space. A model does not need to predict every pixel of the next video frame to understand physics. It only needs to predict a compact, structured representation of what will happen. This forces the network to extract the truly meaningful structure of the world, much like how the human brain works.

LeCun published the foundational vision in his 2022 position paper "A Path Towards Autonomous Machine Intelligence" . Meta AI has since demonstrated JEPA variants such as V-JEPA (video understanding without labels) and I-JEPA (image understanding), showing the architecture learns richer and more transferable representations than transformer-based generative models.

Afya eHealth and JEPA Research

We are actively exploring how JEPA-inspired architectures can be applied to medical image understanding in low-resource settings. Current deep learning models for skin cancer detection are trained on large labelled datasets that are difficult and costly to obtain in the DRC. JEPA's self-supervised, world-model approach offers a promising path to building diagnostic AI that learns from far fewer labelled examples, generalises across different skin types and imaging conditions, and builds a structured representation of lesion morphology rather than pattern-matching against training data. This research direction is central to our long-term vision of accessible, robust AI diagnostics for Sub-Saharan Africa.
