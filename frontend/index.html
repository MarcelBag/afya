Why Artificial Intelligence for Skin Cancer?

In 2019, pioneers Yann LeCun, Geoffrey Hinton , and Yoshua Bengio received the Turing Award for their breakthroughs in neural networks, driving today's AI revolution.

Early and accurate diagnosis is the key to beating deadly diseases like cancer. Yet, traditional medical diagnostics especially for skin cancer remain challenging, subjective, and often inaccessible in many parts of the world.

  • Each year, over 10 million people die of cancer globally.
  • Skin cancer rates are rising, with early detection critical for survival
  • AI can analyze skin images rapidly, consistently, and objectively.

By harnessing AI, we can empower doctors and patients with faster, more accurate, and more widely accessible tools — helping save lives and transform healthcare for everyone, everywhere.

From Turing's Test to Modern Medical AI

In 1950, Alan Turing imagined a test to see if a machine could imitate a human.

AI's founders, including John McCarthy and Marvin Minsky, built early neural network machines — paving the way for today's deep learning breakthroughs.

In healthcare, predictive AI helps anticipate risks before they happen and enables early detection of diseases such as cancer, diabetes, or even new pandemics.
